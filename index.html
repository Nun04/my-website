Logistics Cost Categories

Logistics Cost refers to the costs incurred from various activities within logistics operations aimed at efficient product and service delivery, with customer satisfaction in mind.

1. Transportation Costs

These costs arise from transportation activities, which vary depending on customer needs, product type, transportation method, and distance.

2. Warehousing Costs

These costs come from activities within a warehouse, such as receiving goods, storage, inventory management, and labor costs associated with handling goods.

3. Inventory Carrying Costs

These costs are related to maintaining inventory, including opportunity costs, storage costs, insurance, taxes, and risks such as product damage or theft.

4. Administration Costs

These costs are associated with customer service, order processing, and information systems. They cover expenses such as processing customer orders, managing returns, and the cost of administrative overhead.
